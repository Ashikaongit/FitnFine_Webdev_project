Early Warning Signs & Symptoms

Not sure if you or someone you know is living with mental health problems?
Experiencing one or more of the following feelings or behaviors can be an early warning sign of a problem:

  • Eating or sleeping too much or too little
  • Pulling away from people and usual activities
  • Having low or no energy
  • Feeling numb or like nothing matters
  • Feeling helpless or hopeless
  • Smoking, drinking, or using drugs more than usual
  • Feeling unusually confused, forgetful, on edge, angry, upset, worried, or scared
  • Yelling or fighting with family and friends
  • Experiencing severe mood swings that cause problems in relationships
  • Having persistent thoughts and memories you can't get out of your head
  • Hearing voices or believing things that are not true
  • Thinking of harming yourself or others
  • Inability to perform daily tasks like taking care of your kids or getting to work or school

What can you do?

Anxiety

When anxiety strikes, try deep breathing exercises, mindfulness, and grounding techniques to regain control of your thoughts and emotions. Seek therapy to identify and address the root causes of your anxiety.

Depression

Counteract depression by setting achievable daily goals, engaging in physical activity, and maintaining a routine. Reach out to friends and family for support and consider therapy or medication if necessary.

Bipolar Disorder

For bipolar disorder, adhere to prescribed medication regimens and therapy sessions. Keep a mood diary to monitor patterns and triggers, and involve loved ones in your treatment journey.

Substance Abuse and Addiction

Begin your recovery journey with professional help in rehabilitation programs. Attend counseling and support groups for ongoing sobriety maintenance.

General Stress and Coping

Manage stress by practicing mindfulness, setting realistic goals, and maintaining a healthy work-life balance. Seek support from friends and loved ones, and don't hesitate to consult a therapist when needed.

Low Self-Esteem

Boost your self-esteem by focusing on self-compassion and practicing positive self-talk. Challenge negative beliefs about yourself, set achievable goals, and engage in activities that make you feel competent and valued.

Procrastination

Overcome procrastination by breaking tasks into smaller, manageable steps and setting specific deadlines. Eliminate distractions, create a structured to-do list, and reward yourself for completing tasks on time.

Anger Management

When dealing with anger, practice deep breathing and relaxation techniques. Develop effective communication skills to express your feelings constructively, and consider anger management counseling or therapy to address underlying issues.

Loneliness

To combat loneliness, actively seek social connections by joining clubs, volunteering, or participating in activities you enjoy. Reconnect with old friends and make an effort to reach out to new people. Quality relationships can alleviate feelings of loneliness.

Perfectionism

Address perfectionism by setting realistic standards for yourself. Embrace the concept of "good enough" and allow for mistakes. Practice self-compassion and acknowledge that perfection is unattainable and unnecessary for personal growth.
